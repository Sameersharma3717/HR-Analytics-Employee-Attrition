# 📊 HR Analytics Employee Attrition Analysis

## 📌 Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition using SQL, Python, Excel, and Power BI to uncover workforce trends, identify key factors influencing employee turnover, and support data-driven HR decision-making.

The project follows a complete data analytics workflow, including data cleaning, SQL analysis, exploratory data analysis (EDA), interactive dashboards, and business recommendations.

---

## 🎯 Business Problem

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations as it impacts productivity, recruitment costs, and employee morale.

The objective of this project is to:

- Analyze employee demographics and workforce distribution
- Measure the organization's attrition rate
- Identify departments and job roles with high attrition
- Study the impact of overtime and business travel on employee turnover
- Compare average monthly income across departments
- Provide actionable business recommendations for HR teams

# 💡 Key Business Insights

- The organization has **1,470 employees** with an overall **attrition rate of 16.12%**.
- Research & Development recorded the highest employee attrition.
- Employees working overtime experienced higher attrition than employees without overtime.
- Sales Executives, Research Scientists, and Laboratory Technicians represent the largest workforce segments.
- Most employees fall within the lower to middle monthly income range.
- The Sales department has the highest average monthly income.
- Frequent business travel is associated with higher employee attrition.
- The workforce consists of approximately **60% male** and **40% female** employees.

---

# 🚀 Business Recommendations

- Implement retention strategies in departments with high attrition.
- Reduce excessive overtime by improving workload distribution.
- Review compensation strategies to improve employee retention.
- Enhance employee engagement and career development programs.
- Monitor employees with frequent business travel and provide work-life balance initiatives.
